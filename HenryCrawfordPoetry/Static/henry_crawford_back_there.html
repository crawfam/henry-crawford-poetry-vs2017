Back There

Sometimes a leaf
will leave it's shape
on the chalk white surface
of a sidewalk.
The way a discarded print
will leave its frame
on the wall behind it,
or a rug will make a box
on the varnished wood
beneath it.

It's same way faces
leave their colors
on the matte surface
of a photograph.
Or the way a room
will hold the warmth
of a gathering
after everyone has left.
Walk into that room
still breathing.

And this is the problem
left by my mother
in a room full of machines
with the numbers
running down
and screens going blank.
And then she was dead.
Her arrangement of atoms
alive seconds earlier
were now mapped
in a way that told us
and the machines
there was no
going back.

But what exactly
is back there?

I had a toy car
and a train. I looked up
and you were there.


Henry Crawford
2/7/2020
