an Interactive Poem by Henry Crawford

"The launch of OxyContin tablets

The first emergency room comes hushed
with words like Oxycodone, overdose
and Naloxone whispered behind closed
curtains of hospital white anxiety.
The first time you hear it, you say to yourself
at least he's still alive.

The first time you notice his guitar is missing
you say he must have lent it to a friend.
So you're shaken when you see it
in the pawn shop window with your wife's
missing ring which you discreetly purchase
so you can put it all back.

The last emergency room comes as a closed door.
