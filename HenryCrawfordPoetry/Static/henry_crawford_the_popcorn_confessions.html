The Popcorn Confessions

Sometimes I'll walk into a 1940's cartoon
with a dog nose and orange webbed feet
white gloved hand pointing an enormous finger
at you "Yes, you!" but in a late 70's art film
I'm just a voice writing the first lines of the script
in the sky above a black & white city montage
then showing my joker face with a make-believe
question "Why so serious?" until caught blushing
by an undead Marilyn Monroe her flame unfurled
above a netherworld grate turning me into
a 1939 cowboy pursuing a fake tribe of soundstage
Indians galloping in circles around an indifferent
Monument Valley when suddenly shocked into
a postmodern vision of ironic characters
violently beating each other to a fictional pulp
or remembering a mustachioed Hitler
floating the globe like a beachball of pretend
world-dominance or witnessing the glamor
of two beautiful people from the 1960's
getting machine gunned down in a V8 Ford.
How are our lives any more real than our
dreams? Falling somewhere into an imaginary
Russia on a rodeo rigged atom bomb
or searching for our own red sled
in a pile of artificial snow or left stranded
outside the sanctum of the next godfather
waiting for the doors to close.
