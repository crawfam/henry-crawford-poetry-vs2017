The Ingredients of Coca-Cola
by Henry Crawford

Carbonated Water

In the well at the bottom of my hydrocarbon bosom there are no frontiers but fortresses of multi-layered cell cities submerged in dark compartments under the skin. Commands pouring in from the gray papery mind with its gathering clouds of wordless proclamations leaking from the top of the drip drip drip of daily remorse with every element of bone and crust politely conspiring to keep it all inside.

High Fructose Corn Syrup

My cells sing the sugarcoated song of quiet fire avoiding at all costs the chilly melody of cold reflection or the overheated symphony of orchestrated rage, seeking to keep the needle in the middle with just enough energy to prevent a system wide freeze-out but cool enough for the lukewarm chambers of my happy pumping heart.

Caramel Color

We meet again as shades impossible to paint in oil or watercolor and even when you see me you might take me for blue or gray or you might say I’m invisible or some other wavelength in the 24 hour spectrum of flesh and sometimes you might discern the cobalt of my afternoons or the lavender of my day’s last hour when I will ask if you are taking it all down, but mostly wondering even while you’re smiling, what say you of me?

Phosphoric Acid

No phosphorescent light along the black acid rivers of fizzing and spurting ideas and inclinations, the effervescent bubbles of small vanities vying for light at the top of the head with each refreshing pop getting its moment of luminescence before going dark in a din of carbonated applause.

Natural Flavors

My grandmother, who never liked to speak, used to say if you want to pull a rabbit out of a hat don’t walk in dressed up like a magician. So, they kept the formula in a vault inside an ordinary bank on an ordinary street in postbellum Atlanta, like most family secrets, meant to decay over the half-life of perceived significance.

Caffeine

Now we got it. Now we want it. Now we’re at the airport. Now it’s the winning ticket. Now the gift. Now the needle. Now the way home. Now it’s the head of the class. Now it’s the dollars. Now it's out in the open. Now it’s back in the womb. Now we go north. Now we go south. Now we’re on the highway driving all night. Now we’re going faster. Faster. Until every single star out there is lit.
