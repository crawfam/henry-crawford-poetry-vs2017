The Visitor

The Alzheimer's doors open blue
and hospital white. She was here
only yesterday. She called me
George Today she thinks I don’t

remember. You have to feel
sorry. You should have heard us
talking for hours. Now I guess
my answers. A penny saved is

money burned? A stitch in time
will give you nine? All around
the table. Faces so strange
Many times it’s hard to say

what I should be seeing. When
the Alzheimer’s doors shut
behind us. Swinging blue
and hospital white. She comes

with my old “Surf’s Up!” shirt.
She wants me to recall. There
was a beach. Miles of ocean waves
blue as the open windows of this

dayroom. She’s on her way now.
Doors go blue and hospital white.
But whose pursed kiss? I tighten
my eyes. Whose hand waving?
