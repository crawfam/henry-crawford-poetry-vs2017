Leaving

In my last days in that town I stole a bike
right outside the Rite-Aid. I took it
to the places I wanted to remember
like a dog marking out old ground.

To the junk yard cars where I first
almost got laid. Then the  fort we built
in back of the burned-down house
where I finally did.

I didn't want to see the people
just the things. There would be
new people. They just come at you.
You can't tell why. They just do.

I went to the school yard
to look between the chain links.
Spring days of outdoor gym,
partying by the handball wall.

Past the artificial pond to the Sears
of my commission-salesman dad.
Then on to see the rainwater draining
off the worn stores of Main Street.

I pedaled up to Lookout Hill
a year since I'd made up my mind
to move on. With nowhere else to go
I left the bike for someone else.
